Dr. Joy Buolamwini

PHOTO FROM GRACE HOPPER CELEBRATION

Dr. Joy Buolamwini, recognized by Fortune Magazine as the "the conscience of the AI revolution", is a computer scientist who has contributed a lot in highlighting the ethics and social consequences of using Artificial Intelligence. She is the founder of the Algorithmic Justice League, a non-profit which strives to create a world with more equitable and accountable technology. Her TED Talk on Algorithm Bias has been viewed over 1 million times on YouTube. Her peer-reviewed thesis as a graduate student at the MIT's Media Lab uncovered the racial and gender bias of facial recognition AI produced by Amazon, Microsoft, IBM and others.

Some of her accomplishments include: Forbes youngest 30 under 30, Wired 25, MIT Tech Review 35 under 35, The Bloomberg 30. She was also featured in the documentary, Coded Bias, which covers her discovery of racial bias in face recognition technology.
